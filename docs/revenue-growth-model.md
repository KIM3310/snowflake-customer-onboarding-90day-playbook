# Revenue Growth Model - snowflake-customer-onboarding-90day-playbook

Updated: 2026-05-30

This note translates the archived proof into a current, ethical revenue path. It keeps the repository's archived status visible while preserving the commercial idea behind the work.

## Model

| Field | Decision |
|---|---|
| Revenue type | Archived proof to diagnostic, workshop, enablement, pilot, or partner package |
| Primary reader or buyer | Snowflake partners, data platform teams, customer success leaders, and system integrators. |
| Wedge | The first 90 days after contract signature decide adoption, cost trust, governance posture, and long-term expansion. |
| Operating note | Sell the revived workflow or advisory package; do not sell an unsupported archive as a live product. |

## Offer Ladder

| Offer | Price assumption | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| 90-day onboarding audit | $5k-$18k | Assess discovery, landing zone, governance, and success-review readiness. |
| Onboarding acceleration package | $25k-$100k | Customize templates and SQL scripts for one customer rollout. |
| Partner enablement license | $2k-$15k/month | Maintain reusable onboarding templates, scripts, and success-review material. |

## Activation Loop

- Traffic source: Snowflake partner content.
- First action: read the README status note and one proof path that can be checked quickly.
- Conversion offer: 90-day onboarding audit.
- Expansion: convert a diagnostic or workshop into a scoped pilot, enablement pack, or maintained partner asset.

## Growth Experiments

- Publish the 90-day readiness checklist
- Create a sample onboarding executive review
- Package templates as a partner enablement offer

## Metrics

- Audit bookings
- Template reuse
- Time-to-first-value
- Partner renewals

## Ethical Boundary

- Do not imply Snowflake endorsement
- Avoid guaranteeing business outcomes without baseline data
- Keep customer-specific implementations private
- Keep archived status visible in sales or portfolio conversations.
- Do not invent users, revenue, endorsements, certifications, or customer traction.
- Use buyer psychology only to reduce confusion and risk, not to pressure users.
